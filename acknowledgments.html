Acknowledgements

I am deeply grateful to Dr. Clinton Nash, my mentor and co-author, who first conceived of the problem that would become the foundation of this research. None of this would have been possible without his mathematical brilliance, guidance, and willingness to take a chance on me. He consistently instilled in me a sharp sense of confidence in my academic abilities by treating me as his peer and trusting me as a contributor to this project. It is this generosity of his that gave me the opportunity to work on the best research project of my life so far.

I would also like to thank Mr. Matthew Amoroso, who not only reviewed our paper and offered insightful feedback, but who has been the source of everything I know about physics. As my AP physics teacher, his encouragement, clarity, and rigor have shaped the way I think about the physical world and have empowered me to pursue this kind of work with confidence.

Dr. Nash and I are indebted to the reviewer(s) at the Journal of Undergraduate Research in Physics and Astronomy (JURPA), whose thoughtful suggestions on structure and mathematical notation greatly improved the clarity and accessibility of the paper. Their guidance helped shape our ideas into a form that could speak more clearly and precisely to a broader audience.

Lastly, I am immensely thankful to Mr. Ben Grasso and Thornton Academy, who purchased my Society of Physics Students membership so that I could submit our paper to JURPA in the first place. Their kindness and belief in this project made it possible for our work to reach a larger academic community.
